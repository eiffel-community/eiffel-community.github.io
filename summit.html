Eiffel Summit 2023.1

This in-person two-day conference for people interested in the Eiffel protocol and its ecosystem will have a mix of presentations and discussions. Newcomers as well as experienced practitioners are welcome.

The practicalities

  • Where: doWhile, Lindholmspiren 7, 417 56 Gothenborg, Sweden
  • When: June 13, 09:00 CEST, 2023, to June 14, 17:00 CEST (exact start and end times tentative)
  • Who: To get in touch with the organizers send an email to eiffel-tc@googlegroups.com.
  • Communication: For questions, comments, or suggestions regarding the Summit, please join us on Slack.
  • Registration: This event is free, but please register no later than June 5 to allow us to plan the event.

Agenda

The agenda is still being worked on, but we're gravitating towards these themes (partially dependent on the indicated interest of people who register):

Improved source change events
The current source change events model an abstraction of what goes on in a source code repository. This is sufficient for some use cases but can pose problems if you want a model that more closely matches reality. Let's continue the discussion around this and the concrete proposal in github.com/eiffel-community/eiffel#261 . That aside, with the current events there's no standard way of representing tags.
Deployment events and DORA metrics
What can we do with the experimental EiffelArtifactDeployedEvent (github.com/eiffel-community/eiffel#322)? Which DORA metrics can we cover with Eiffel?
What is Eiffel and why should I care?
Introduction to those who are new to Eiffel.
Visualizations
How can we use Eiffel to visualize CI/CD pipeline, either with generic solutions or highly specialized ones.
Security, CVEs, and SBOMs
How can Eiffel be used to address concerns related to security and software supply chains?

This page will be updated as we get closer to the date.

The second day of the summit, June 14, is adjacent to the Software Center reporting workshop, and the afternoon of this day aims to cater to a wider audience of people who may not be familar with Eiffel in particular but is well-versed in the CI/CD space in general, possibly from the academic side.

If there's any topic you'd like to see covered please reach out to us.
